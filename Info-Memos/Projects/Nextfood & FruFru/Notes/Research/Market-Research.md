## 6. Consumer Trends Driving the Segment

**Health consciousness:**
- 35% of Romanian consumers rate health as excellent/very good
- ~65% more conscious about dietary choices than 5 years ago
- 62% cite health benefits as main motivation for buying locally produced food

**Economic fundamentals:**
- GDP per capita: USD 18,853 (2024) → USD 19,585 (2025)
- Income convergence: from 26% of EU average (2000) to 78% (2024)
- Urban population: 54.88% (2024) — below Western Europe ~75-80% → further urbanization runway
- Food expenditure: 24.8% of household budget (highest in EU vs. 8-12% in West) → shift toward convenience/premium as incomes rise

**Digital adoption:**
- E-commerce spending: EUR 11.7B (2024, +10% YoY)
- Online food delivery user penetration: 19.2% (2024), growing

**Demographic drivers:**
- Growing urban middle class (Bucharest, Cluj, Timisoara, Iasi, Constanta)
- Under-40, educated, urban professionals = core healthy food demographic
- Corporate wellness programs expanding (IT/gaming early adopters for B2B)

## 7. European Context — Romania vs. Western Europe (Penetration Gap)

**Per capita ready meals comparison (2025, Statista):**

| Country | Per Capita Revenue | Per Capita Volume |
|---------|-------------------|-------------------|
| **Romania** | **USD 100.80** | **15.1 kg** |
| UK | ~USD 89 | 6.3 kg |
| France | — | 14.3 kg |
| **Central & Western Europe avg** | **USD 134.30** | **15.74 kg** |

Romania's per capita volume (15.1 kg) is comparable to France — but per capita revenue (USD 100.80) is below C&W European average (USD 134.30). **Room for premiumization.**

**The convergence thesis:**
1. **Income convergence:** Romania at 78% of EU average income (up from 26% in 2000)
2. **Convenience food adoption lag:** Home-cooking preference eroding with urbanization, dual-income households, younger generations
3. **Healthy food premiumization:** Europe's healthy/organic food market: USD 213.47B (2024) → USD 488.79B by 2033 (CAGR 9.64%). Romania at early stage.
4. **Online meal delivery underpenetration:** Romania ~USD 532M vs. multi-billion in UK/Germany/France
5. **European healthy foods CAGR:** 8.3-9.6% (2024-2031) — outgrowing base food market everywhere

**Investment thesis summary:**
Romania at intersection of three convergence trends: (a) income convergence toward EU averages, (b) convenience food adoption following Eastern→Western European pattern, (c) global health-consciousness wave. Market is fragmented (no dominant healthy prepared meals player at scale), subscale, and structurally underpenetrated. Classic PE scale-up opportunity.
